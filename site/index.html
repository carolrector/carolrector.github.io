    Blacksburg Eats

    Welcome to Blacksburg Eats, your go-to guide for navigating the local food scene without the guesswork.

    Whether you are new to town or just looking to switch things up, this guide is built to help you find exactly what you are craving, when you need it. From quick, affordable bites to sit-down spots perfect for a night out, Blacksburg has more variety than you might expect—and we have organized it to make exploring easy.

    You will find recommendations tailored to different moods and moments when you are trying to stick to a budget grabbing something late at night, staying on top of your health goals, or planning a date or group outing. Each category highlights places that stand out not just for their food, but for their overall experience, whether that means portion size, atmosphere, speed, or value.

    Beyond restaurants, we have also included a few local staples and specialty spots worth knowing about, from international markets to beloved grab-and-go favorites. Think of this guide as a curated starting point, reliable, student-friendly, and designed to help you make the most of eating in Blacksburg.

    When You're ... On A Budget

    Eating well in Blacksburg does not have to mean spending a lot. There are plenty of spots around town that offer generous portions, solid quality, and prices that will not hurt your wallet.

    Whether you are trying to stretch a weekly budget or just want the most food for your money, these places deliver consistency and value. Expect quick service, filling meals, and menus built with students in mind.

    From comfort food to international flavors, budget-friendly does not mean boring—it just means you know where to go to get the most out of every dollar.

    Souvelaki ($)

    201 College Ave, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Offers generous portions at budget-friendly prices, ideal for college students.
    • Large subs are priced at $11 or less and are packed-full to say the least.
    • Menu includes curly fries as a standout side option.

    El Rodeo ($)

    623 N Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    1540 S Main St Ste 100, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Your reliable, go-to Mexican restaurant offering large portions with complimentary chips and salsa.
    • Features daily specials on both food and drinks.
    • Provides fast and efficient service and can quickly accommodate large parties.

    Great spot for a celebration with friends!

    Macado's ($)

    922 University City Blvd, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Funky, cool restaurant vibe with a variety of daily specials throughout the week.
    • Best known for its 39-cent wings on Thursdays.
    • Good place to watch sports or bring big parties as they have a large dining room/sports bar.

    When You're ... Looking for a Late Night Bite

    Late nights in Blacksburg almost always end with food, and luckily, there are a few reliable spots that stay open when everything else is closed. Whether you are heading home after a night out or just up late and hungry, these places are known for fast service, satisfying portions, and hours that go well past midnight. Expect classic comfort foods, easy grab-and-go options, and menus designed to hit the spot when you need it most. These are the go-to locations that keep the town fed long after the evening winds down.

    Bennys ($)

    110 Draper Rd NW, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Blacksburg classic pizza spot with huge slices, and a constant line out the door.
    • It closes at 12 AM weekdays, and 2 AM on weekends.
    • Home of the first original Benny’s created by Hokie alums before they expanded down the east coast.

    Get an extra garlic or ranch dipping sauce, as well as hot honey on your slice!

    DP Dough ($)

    215 N Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • A fast, classic calzone place in the heart of downtown Blacksburg that closes at 4 AM.
    • Best for a quick grab on your way home.

    Cookout ($)

    1311 S Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • In-store and drive through service for trays of fried food, burgers, milkshakes, and more.
    • Open through 3:30 AM most days and 4:30 AM on Fridays.

    When You're ... On Your Health Grind

    If you are trying to eat clean without sacrificing flavor, Blacksburg has options that make it easy to stay on track. These spots focus on fresh ingredients, customizable meals, and balanced portions that leave you feeling full but not weighed down. Whether you are building bowls, salads, or wraps, the emphasis is on choice and quality. Perfect for quick lunches, post-workout meals, or everyday eating, these places prove that “healthy” does not have to mean bland. It is all about finding meals that fuel you while still tasting good enough to keep coming back.

    Mezeh ($$)

    616 University City Blvd, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Create customizable Mediterranean bowls, wraps, and salads with fresh ingredients.
    • Known for generous portions, healthy options, and a quick build-your-own dining experience.

    And you get a free slice of warm, chewy pita bread with every meal.

    Roots ($$)

    898 Prices Fork Rd, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Create your own American style bowls based on clean ingredients.
    • Walking distance from campus so very easily accessible for freshman.

    Author's Note: the El Jefe "with the works" is their best signature bowl.

    Chipotle ($)

    314 N Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    868 Prices Fork Rd, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Quick and easy.
    • Usually has a long line during regular meal hours.
    • Has a reward program for free food and one specifically for university students.

    When You're ... On a Date

    Finding the right spot for a date in Blacksburg is all about the atmosphere as much as the food. Whether you are planning something casual or a little more elevated, there are restaurants that strike the perfect balance between comfort and quality. Think cozy interiors, shareable plates, and menus that offer something for everyone. These places are ideal for conversation, connection, and making a good impression without feeling over the top. From laid-back dinners with your friends to more polished experiences, these are the go-to spots when you want the setting to match the moment.

    622 North ($$$)

    622 N Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Classic sit-down restaurant with a varied selection of nicer food like duck, steak, and ravioli, and more laid-back options like burgers and sandwiches.
    • Great for after a big event or on a special occasion.

    Greens ($$)

    109 College Ave, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Local sushi restaurant downtown that has something for everyone: from sashimi to poke bowls to fried food.
    • The spicy pork dumplings appetizer is a fan favorite!
    • They also serve some mediterranean, mexican, and american food as well (Tacos, burgers, pitas, hummus plates, etc).

    For avid sushi lovers, and Greens is your go-to! They have great deals on Mondays: 2 rolls for $10.99!

    Wine Lab ($$$)

    223 Gilbert St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Good for dates or small groups with a cozy, shareable-plate vibe.
    • Deals: happy hour + occasional specials and wine club perks!

    Honorable Mentions

    Not every great food spot fits neatly into one category, and that is where these honorable mentions come in. This mix highlights some of Blacksburg’s most reliable and underrated staples, from international grocery stores and organic markets to quick takeout favorites and local institutions. Whether you are looking to cook something new at home, grab something fast and affordable, or try a place locals swear by, these spots round out the food scene in a meaningful way. They each offer something a little different, but all are worth knowing—especially once you start exploring beyond the usual go-to restaurants.

    Specialty Grocery:

    Oasis World Market

    1411 S Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Offers a wide selection of Asian and international groceries.
    • Carries unique snacks, spices, and hard-to-find ingredients.
    • Great spot for cooking authentic global dishes at home.

    The Natural Food Co-Op

    708 N Main St #A, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Small organic grocery store with a local, community feel.
    • Sells crystals, trinkets, and goods from local artists.
    • Focuses on natural, sustainable, and eco-friendly products.
    • It has a small fridge section near the cheese area, where there are snack portions of cheese, daily made vegetarian sandwiches, and grab and go stuff.

    ALDI

    2265 N Franklin St, Christiansburg, VA 24073

    • Budget-friendly grocery store with consistently low prices.
    • Requires bringing your own bags for a streamlined checkout and a 'security deposit' quarter to borrow a cart while you are shopping.
    • Mostly store-brand items that are cheaper than major chains.
    • They do not carry many popular brands.

    More Restaurants:

    Carol Lee Donuts

    1414 N Main St, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • A classic Blacksburg staple loved by locals.
    • Known for fresh, simple, and consistently good donuts.
    • Cheap, quick, and perfect for an easy treat.

    Himalayan Curry Cafe

    781 University City Blvd, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Serves flavorful Himalayan and Indian-inspired dishes.
    • Known for generous portions and comforting meals.

      Pro Tip: go before closing on Mondays for possible extra food because they aren't open on Tuesdays and need to get rid of everything left over.

    Chinese Kitchen

    1409 N Main St No.002, Blacksburg, VA 24060

    • Reliable takeout spot with great value for the price.
    • Portions are large and ideal for sharing with friends.

      Fan favorite is the crab rangoons, with about 10 pieces for a low cost.

    Conclusion

    In the end, Blacksburg Eats serves as more than just a list of places to eat—it’s a practical guide designed to simplify everyday decisions while helping you get more out of the local food scene. Whether you are prioritizing affordability, convenience, health, or atmosphere, this guide brings together a range of options that reflect both the diversity and accessibility of dining in Blacksburg. It highlights not only where to go, but why each place stands out, making it easier to match your choice with your needs in the moment.

    What makes this guide especially valuable is its focus on experience. From late-night staples to date-night destinations and hidden gems, it encourages exploration beyond routine habits and introduces spots that you might not have considered otherwise. It also reinforces that good food in Blacksburg does not have to be expensive or complicated—it just requires knowing where to look.

    Ultimately, this guide is a starting point. As you try new places and revisit favorites, it becomes a tool for building your own go-to list and discovering what makes the local food scene uniquely yours.
